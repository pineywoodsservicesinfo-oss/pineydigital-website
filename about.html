About

I build the systems that stop the operational bleed.

When your business runs on spreadsheets, phone calls, and paper logs, you're losing time, money, and clarity. I build the Operational OS that replaces chaos with control.

Why I understand operational chaos

I didn't start in tech. I worked in restaurants, construction, landscaping — the kind of jobs where you see how businesses actually run. Where you watch managers track inventory on paper, lose entire afternoons to phone-tag, and leak profit through the cracks of disconnected systems.

That's where I learned what operational friction looks like. Not from a textbook — from watching business owners drown in tasks that should have been automated years ago.

Why Piney Digital exists

Most businesses don't need another website. They need a central nervous system — a single place where operations, data, and decisions connect. They need to stop the bleeding from manual processes, paper logs, and tools that don't talk to each other.

That's what I build. Not marketing sites, but Operational OS platforms — custom systems that unify your locations, automate the repetitive, and surface the insights you've been missing.

Who I work with

Multi-location businesses. Growing enterprises. Operations teams tired of duct-taping spreadsheets together. If you've ever thought "there has to be a better way to run this," you're in the right place.

I'm based in Texas, but I serve businesses everywhere. Remote-first, relationship-driven — your success is my portfolio.

What makes this different

Most developers deliver code and walk away. You get a finished product, and then you're on your own for every update, bug, and new feature.

I do it differently. Building together is just the beginning. Your initial project includes training, documentation, and 90 days of support. After that, you have options: manage it yourself, or continue with ongoing care — hosting, maintenance, and a developer who already knows your system inside and out.

That's the difference between buying software and having an Operational OS that grows with your business.
